Amazon Bot

Overview:

This was a small project I created for myself during spring break (2021). I used my time to study-up
on Selenium and a bit of python to make it work. For context: GPU’s, graphical processing units, at the
time were heavily out of stock. And whenever one got restocked, it sold out almost immediately. So, in
order to snag just one, I created a very simple and very-much flawed bot (it worked actually worked).

Contribution:

The bot essentially will open a browser instance, open the provided Amazon page and will refresh every one second
until the “add to cart” option is available. Once clicked, it will enter valid Amazon credentials and submit the order.

Complications:

- The major counter to this solution is Amazon’s bot detection. Once a CAPTCHA popped up, the bot would keep refreshing that page, defeating the purpose.

- This solution is specifically tailored to Amazon. Different websites have different page elements, which would call for the whole program to be rewritten.
